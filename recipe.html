Ethiopian Doro Wat

Doro Wat is Ethiopia’s signature dish: a rich, spicy chicken stew simmered in a flavorful sauce of onions, garlic, ginger, and berbere spice mix.
It is traditionally served with injera, Ethiopia’s beloved sour flatbread, and often enjoyed during holidays and special occasions.

Recipe Information

Preparation Time: 20 minutes

Cooking Time: 1 hour 15 minutes

Servings: 6 people

Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 4 large onions, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup niter kibbeh (Ethiopian spiced butter)
  • 3 tbsp berbere spice blend
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2–3 cups chicken broth or water
  • 6 boiled eggs (peeled)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large heavy pot, slowly cook the onions without oil until soft and golden brown. This step builds the base flavor.
  2. Add the niter kibbeh and stir in the garlic and ginger. Cook for 2–3 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Mix in the berbere spice blend and tomato paste, stirring well to form a rich, red sauce.
  4. Add the chicken pieces, coating them thoroughly in the sauce. Cook for about 10 minutes to absorb flavor.
  5. Pour in the chicken broth, reduce heat, and let the stew simmer gently for 45–60 minutes until chicken is tender.
  6. Season with salt and pepper. Add the boiled eggs during the last 10 minutes of cooking so they soak up the flavors.
  7. Serve hot with injera or rice, and enjoy this Ethiopian classic!

Nutrition Facts (per serving)

  • Calories: ~420 kcal
  • Protein: 32g
  • Fat: 28g
  • Carbohydrates: 12g
  • Sodium: 580mg

Tips

For authentic flavor, always use niter kibbeh and Ethiopian berbere spice blend. If you prefer a milder version, reduce the amount of berbere.
Traditionally, Doro Wat is eaten with injera using your hands—no utensils needed!
